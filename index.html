Professional History

2004

Since I was interested in everything IT I started training as Information Technology Assistant at Technische Schulen des Kreises Steinfurt Berufskolleg in Steinfurt. This training introducted me to basic knowledge of computer science and electrical engineering and production conveys. My favorite classes were programming and databases, signalizing my career to be.

2005

In our spare time Dennis Lohaus and I started developing a browser-based game named Memania. This very project was the foundation for me to learn the basic concepts of web-puplishing, web-design and scripting with PHP.

2006

I shut down my training as ITA and started a new training as Management assistant in advertising at the web marketing agency dimento.com in Münster.

2007

The popular German social bookmarking service LinkARENA owned by dimento.com was completely redesigned. I was the frontend developer, helping to design the UI and implementing the templates in HTML, CSS and Javascript on the foundation of a Zend-based backend.
We led LinkARENA to be the second-most popular service of this kind in Germany.

I also played around with Ruby on Rails in my spare time. My first noteworthy project was a Social Feed Reader named “Social Reader”. There is still a video about Social Reader available on Vimeo. My friends at Zweitag provided me the required infrastructure for it.

2008

As soon as my former boss at dimento.com learned about my knowlegde of Ruby on Rails he started to put me in charge to develop a new version of the company’s CMS dimento.MEDIA. It never got out of the prototype phase because I left the job to start my career at Trusted Shops.

2009

I was hired by Trusted Shops in Cologne because of my PHP skills. Pretty soon I was set in charge of development and release management of the Trusted Shops website.

2010

After a couple of months the company’s management decided to let me develop a new web-application for the research team. It managed the contact preparation for the sales department. Since the project had gone well, I was chosen to develop the complete sales process on top of the existing application. Project “EVA” was born.

2011

After many months of designing, developing and iterating EVA was released as an internal web-application to be used by almost all departments of Trusted Shops.

2012

By now EVA contains and manages the information of all potential and actual customers/clients of the company. Sales employees record the progress with their leads, the membership management department confirm new customers, the legal department reviews legal matters of potential clients, the managament controls contact assignment and reviews revenues, ...
